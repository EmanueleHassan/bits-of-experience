This comes from the property of shadow prices in linear programming. The fact is that in these economics books they do not focus on the math but to its application to the specific subject and interpret the result based on the exercise at hand.

Well, that might make sense because you can understand some notions of the subject at hand in such a way. This is the ultimate goal in applied math.

But if you understand the general math behind it you then have a general frame that you can apply to different subjects. This is why you are interested in the general understanding of the math theory behind it.

In 1909, Tel-Aviv was born. "Tel Aviv" was the title of the Hebrew translation of Herzl's utopian novel Altneuland.

A tel is a hill created by the remains of homes and buildings of many generations of people who had lived and rebuilt communities on the same location. With time, the level of the hill rises, creating a mound that can be excavated layer by layer to discover the various stages of life there. Tel was a reference to the past. Aviv is the Hebrew word for "spring". Aviv, therefore, captured the Old-New Land to which Herzl referred in Altneuland.
